4. Important Legal Notice: Free, Cost-Sharing and Paid Rides

Users must understand the difference between free rides, cost-sharing rides and paid rides.

A Free Ride is a voluntary lift where the Driver receives no payment, reward or contribution.

A Cost-Sharing Ride is a private arrangement where the Passenger contributes only towards actual costs such as fuel, parking or toll fees.

A Paid Ride may be treated as passenger transport for reward and may require licences, permits, public operating authorisation, professional driving permits, insurance and compliance with transport laws.

South African law regulates public transport and e-hailing services, and paid passenger transport may require operating licences. The National Department of Transport has indicated that e-hailing is recognised as a transport service type and that operators must comply with applicable licensing requirements.

Each Driver is solely responsible for ensuring that they comply with all laws, permits, licences, insurance requirements and vehicle requirements before offering any ride, especially any paid ride.

Duranki and Inkolo Connect do not guarantee that any Driver is legally authorised to provide paid transport.
